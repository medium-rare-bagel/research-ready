Honest answer on what went wrong:

The three passes I ran were against my own output, not against the source document. I was checking "did I describe the items I included accurately" rather than "did I include everything in the wishlist." That's a completeness check failure, not an accuracy check failure.

The root cause: by the time I was running passes, I had already mentally closed the loop on what items existed. I wasn't re-reading the wishlist line by line — I was reviewing what I'd already written.

The two missing items were also in a section I had partly covered (Enhancements to Existing Commands), which made the gap harder to notice than if they'd been in a section I skipped entirely.

*How to phrase it more effectively:*

Instead of "compare to the repo WISHLIST.md and refine," be explicit that each pass should verify completeness against the source:

"After drafting, do three passes. Each pass: read the source WISHLIST.md top to bottom, check every item is represented in the table, and correct anything missing or inaccurate. Don't just review what you wrote — verify against the source."

The key addition is "read the source top to bottom" — that forces a line-by-line comparison rather than a self-review. Without that, I'll naturally check my own work against my own memory of the source, which is circular.
